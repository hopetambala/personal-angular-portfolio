Framing the Problem

There are five things that directly impact a user's willingness to work out: 1) tiredness as a result of no sleep, 2) time of day (is the gym closed/close to closing?), 3) proximity to the gym (or a place to work out), 4) weight gain and visibly seeing poor health and 5) seeing results of a work-out. Interestingly enough, four of these causes happen before a user works out, and one of them occurs after.

The Stronger App is designed to address issues with the time of day a user can work out, proximity to the gym (or a place to work out) and seeing results of a work-out. It's solely designed with the philosophy of "I want to be stronger than I was yesterday." It's geared towards always improving no matter how big or small those improvements are. It incorporates elements of goal planning/setting, work-out reminding, social support, and self-monitoring.

CHALLENGE

Professionals are too busy to schedule effective workouts

There’s a lot that goes into effective workouts and busy users don’t have the time to research and plan the appropriate workouts for themselves.

SOLUTION

An AI-assisted workout planner

An AI-assisted application that creates a workout plan based on 1) the busyness of the users schedule, 2) location of the user, 3) and the time of day.

Overview and Causal Pathways

A causal loop diagram is a causal diagram that aids in visualizing how different variables in a system are interrelated.

When we look at the following figure, we see that five major factors affect if a user is going to work-out. There are uncontrollable factors such as time of day and relatively uncontrollable factors such as proximity to gym. With Stronger as the behavioral change technology, we attempt to address time of day and proximity to the gym by having our Stronger Assistant consider those factors in choosing the types of workouts the user will accomplish.

Low-Fidelity Prototyping

Through the design of this application, I went through a couple iterations of how I wanted to scope the application. For example, Stronger is focused on strength-based workouts and workout planning has an intentional calendar week “framing”.


There’s “reinforcement loop“ between a 1) user seeing results and 2) desiring tailored results to see better results. Having a specifically created regiment for the user at their exercise level, weight, and age would allow for the appropriately assigned workouts. We chose to break down workouts on work-out types, sets within those work-out types and then reps within those sets.


Goal attainability is also affected by the time-of-day and location of the user. The chatbot is designed to ask for work-out area preferences. If a user is at work and there's a gym there, the user marks the location. If the user is at home (like an apartment complex) and there's a gym there, they mark it. If the user's at home and there's no gym, the chatbot would prompt the user (via a push notification) if they wanted to shuffle and change pre-planned exercise activities to more home-appropriate exercises.


By adding these tailored features and thinking critically about goal planning and attainability, we lower the barrier of entry into completing tasks and potentially increase the user's motivation to work out.
