UX Designer + Software Developer

Framing the Problem

When I was a Peace Corps Volunteer, other volunteers and I used to be medical translators for American medical missions who would come down to the Dominican Republic. The more we worked with these groups, the more disheartened we became because they while they were passionate and willing, they were inefficient with their work; using paper records that would just end up in boxes that they wouldn't look at again.

Their work wasn't sustainable due to no user-research involved in the planning of initiatives.

I started Puente with other Peace Corps Volunteers to become tech consultants for nonprofits (providing an app for them to collect information that would be available for them and other groups) and social workers for the various communities being worked in(using the majority of money donated to us to do projects for communities with the data that is being collected)

CHALLENGE

Data Collection is paper-based for medical missions

Nonprofit don't collect data that is easily interoperable with other data and is often paper-based. This makes it hard for data collected to have more than a one-time use

SOLUTION

A data collection application designed for streamlined interoperable data

I developed a mobile applicaiton to streamline data collection in developing countries by providing forms that adhere to data most organizations need to make an impact in a community

Prototyping and Testing

Version 1

Version 1 of the application was very straightforward and was early in my frontend/mobile application career. Design choices we had to make were limited to development skill. What we learned from our research was that at a minimum we need A) the app to be bilingual and B) we needed a comprehensive list of questions.

Version 3

Version 1 solved one of our problems. The underlying database that was being created for the information that was collected was exactly what we wanted. However, the user experience was very poor. Version 3 came with the clear goal of addressing the user's pain points with regards to the speed of data collection and enjoyability of the experience. Version 3 (yes we skipped 2 because there was a lot of prototyping and usability testing) was a hit with the users.
