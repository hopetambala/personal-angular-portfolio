Framing the Problem

Problem Statement

To address parking challenges during downtown construction, the city of Ferndale is piloting a program to provide rides to downtown.
This team will dive into ridesharing data and apply the design process to understand and suggest recommendations for mobility decisions

User Research Method: Asset-Based Mapping

When I was a Peace Corps Volunteer, I often found it important to engage community members (i.e. users) with interactive research methods. Valuable research methods such as asset-based mapping translate in the UX process very well, especially in civic tech.

I had the participants explain to me key assets in their "community." They were first tasked with explaining A) how each asset relates to another asset and B) which assets are directly related to the challenge we're trying to crack! This gave us an opportunity to understand how users talk about their community while being engaged in a unique activity!

Data Analysis

Here's one of my interactive exploratory analyses of the Ferndale Population based on a Bikesharing survey conducted by the city. This first analysis was instrumental in understanding the user population of the city.

The information is presented in 4 dimensions: Salary (X-Axis), Number of People Surveyed (Y-Axis), If they work in Ferndale (Coloring of Bars i.e. Yellow vs Green), and Age (Color gradient within each bar i.e. Light-Green to Dark-Green or Light-Yellow to Dark-Yellow)
