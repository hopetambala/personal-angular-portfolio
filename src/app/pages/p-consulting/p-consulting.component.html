User Experience Consulting for Entrepreneurs

Teaching design thinking through workshops and case studies

UX Researcher + UX Strategist

Framing the Problem

Hired as a UX Consultant for the Office of Professional and Client Engagement at the University of Michigan, I was tasked with understanding how to present design thinking to entrepreneurs and entrepreneurial students.

The immediate challenge we ran into was "How do we prove that conscientious design thinking has a measurable business impact?"

We started digging around conducting user interviews with entrepreneurs, designers, seasoned business professionals, and students to find out what sort of material would resonate.

CHALLENGE

Entrepreneurs need to think about design

Design isn't limited to aesthetics. A product regardless of the industry requires rigourous user-centric design

SOLUTION

Provide a series of consulting workshops to leverage design thinking to maximize product impact

I've consulted with established entrepreneurs, business school administrators, and design professionals to provide workshops to package the design process in a digestible manner

UX Consulting

Define

Our workshops required us to develop focused but comprehensive content for an audience of entrepreneurs who were early in their prototypes up to products in their final stages.

Delivering design consulting to empower entrepreneurs to discover their own design flaws creates a work culture of sustainable design.

Key Takeaways

Broad consultations for entrepreneurs is difficult.

We have many clients who work with products and with software. Providing a presentation that addressed both was a challenge, but one we were conscious of after a couple sample workshops.

Case studies are key

In all of our presentations, we make sure to use real-life case studies of work we had done in the past. Being an entrepreneur myself, I was able to tie our design principles with concret examples of products I've designed and software I've developed.

Have fun

Designing is a fun process! Being rigorous in our research was important, but when presenting, relax. People want to come away from workshops feeling confident they can incorporate the design process in their workflows. Inundating them with too much information is ineffective.
